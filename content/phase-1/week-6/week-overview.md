**Brownfield codebase:** [Canvas LMS](https://github.com/instructure/canvas-lms)

## Learning outcomes

By the end of this week, students should be able to:

1. **Explain** why git worktrees are often preferable to long-lived stashes for AI-driven spikes and parallel experiments.
2. **Apply** naming, cleanup, and disk hygiene practices when using multiple worktrees.
3. **Pair** worktrees with distinct AI sessions or rule sets to compare strategies on the same bounded task.
4. **Define** branch policy when AI generates high-churn changes across a worktree.
5. **Compare** two AI strategies on an identical spike and **summarize** tradeoffs without merging blindly.
6. **Produce** a **worktree playbook** tailored to **Canvas LMS** (commands, conventions, teardown).
